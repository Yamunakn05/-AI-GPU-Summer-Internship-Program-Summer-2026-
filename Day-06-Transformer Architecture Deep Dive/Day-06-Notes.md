# Day 06 - Transformer Architecture Deep Dive

## Overview

This session focused on the Transformer architecture, which forms the foundation of modern Large Language Models (LLMs). The concepts of self-attention, positional encoding, and encoder-decoder architecture were explored to understand how transformers process sequential data efficiently.

## Topics Covered

* Introduction to Transformer Architecture
* Encoder and Decoder Architecture
* Self-Attention Mechanism
* Multi-Head Attention
* Positional Encoding
* Feed Forward Neural Networks
* Residual Connections and Layer Normalization
* Applications of Transformers in NLP and Generative AI

## Activities Completed

* Studied the overall architecture of transformer models.
* Explored the working of self-attention and multi-head attention mechanisms.
* Understood how positional encoding preserves sequence information.
* Analyzed the encoder-decoder workflow used in transformer-based models.
* Discussed real-world applications of transformers in AI systems.

## Files Included

* Transformer architecture notebooks
* Attention mechanism exercises
* Practical demonstrations
* Reference materials

## Key Learnings

* Understanding the limitations of traditional RNNs and LSTMs.
* Learning how transformers process information in parallel.
* Understanding the role of self-attention in capturing contextual relationships.
* Importance of positional encoding for sequence modeling.
* Applications of transformer models in machine translation, text generation, summarization, and question answering.

## Outcome

Successfully gained a strong conceptual understanding of Transformer Architecture and its significance in modern Artificial Intelligence, Large Language Models, and Generative AI applications.
